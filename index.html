An Irish Ship in Norwegian Waters

On 9 November 1721, a ship out of Cork, Ireland called The Providentz, bound for Arendal, Norway, sank near the southern Norwegian town of Mandal. Following a court hearing to determine who was at fault, the ship lay forgotten for 260 years. In the 1980s, a member of the Mandal Diving Club found the court proceedings, and for 40 years, the club searched for the ship. In December of 2020, a shipwreck was discovered and the club, along with divers from the Norwegian Maritime Museum, began an investigation.

The history of The Providentz

Out of Ireland

The ship was owned by a wealthy Cork businessman named Joseph Lavit. His family had made their fortune through a number of ventures, including whiskey production, privateering, and as merchants. The Lavits were also well-placed politically, with members of the family serving as Lord Mayors and Aldermen of the city of Cork.

Arrival in Norway

The Providentz set sail from Cork on 3 October, 1721, bound for Arendal, Norway. The ship anchored near Eikvåg, Norway for 6 days, then continued on its way. 2 days later, the ship was forced to anchor outside Mandal. After 13 days, the weather cleared and a local pilot was sent for. The ship began to make way but something went wrong. The ship drew closer and closer to shore until it ran aground and began taking on water. The crew managed to sail the ship to a safe harbour and salvage the rigging before it sank.

An inquiry was called to determine who was at fault. The Irish crew claimed that the pilot was confusing port and starboard. The pilot could not be found for the inquiry, but sent a statement claiming that the crew was drunk. In the end, the blame was placed on the pilot.

Lost to time

After the trial the ship was forgotten until the 1980s when a member of the Mandal Diving Club found the court documents from the inquiry. Divers from the club began searching when they were able, until they found some ship's equipment and exposed ship hull on the sea floor. Believing this to be the remains of The Providentz, the club contacted the Norwegian Maritime Museum. Successive dives showed that they had found what they were looking for.
