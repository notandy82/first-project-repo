On 9 November 1721, a ship out of Cork, Ireland called The Providentz, bound for Arendal, Norway, sank near the southern Norwegian town of Mandal. Following a court hearing to determine who was at fault, the ship lay forgotten for 260 years. A member of the Mandal Diving Club found the court proceedings, and for 40 years, the club searched for the ship. In December of 2020, a shipwreck was discovered and the club, along with divers from the Norwegian Maritime Museum, began an investigation.
